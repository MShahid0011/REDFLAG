About Us

Why We Built Red Flag

Security teams can patch software. They can't patch attention. Social engineering succeeds by targeting judgment in the moment — urgency, authority, trust, and fear rather than a technical flaw. The best defense isn't a firewall; it's practiced recognition.

Most awareness training is a slideshow people forget by lunchtime. We wanted something closer to muscle memory: realistic scenarios, actual decisions to make, and immediate feedback explaining what gave the attack away.

Every scenario is fictional but modeled closely on real, documented attack patterns. The red flags you learn to identify here transfer directly to your inbox, messages, phone calls, and workplace.

Principles

What Guided The Build

01

Realism Over Gimmicks

Every scenario is written to resemble an actual email, text, or phone call — not a quiz-show caricature.

02

Show The Evidence

Feedback highlights the exact clues that exposed the threat, rather than simply saying right or wrong.

03

Legitimate Examples Too

Learning cybersecurity also means recognizing genuine messages. Some scenarios are intentionally safe and trustworthy.

04

No Real Harm, Ever

This is a closed simulation. Nothing sends real messages, contacts real people, or stores personal information.
