Trending right now

Tactics on the rise in 2026

AITM

Adversary-in-the-Middle phishing

Attackers mirror real login flows to capture both a password and the active session cookie, bypassing MFA entirely rather than trying to defeat it directly.

QUISHING

QR-code phishing

Malicious QR codes embedded in emails, fake parking notices, and delivery slips push victims to phishing pages through a phone's browser, where links are harder to inspect.

CALENDAR

Calendar invite phishing

Fake renewal notices and meeting invites are inserted directly into calendar apps, exploiting the trust users place in scheduling tools to slip past email filters.

BEC

Business Email Compromise

Impersonated executives or vendors request urgent wire transfers or updated banking details. The FBI logged $2.77B in reported BEC losses from just over 21,000 complaints in a single year.

DEVICE CODE

Device-code phishing

Attackers abuse legitimate "device login" authentication flows to trick users into approving access without ever stealing a password.
